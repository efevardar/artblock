Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 27, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Art Block (the “Service”) collects, uses, and shares personal information when you use our mobile application. “We”, “us”, and “our” refer to the operator of Art Block described under Who we are below. By using the Service, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

Who we are

Art Block is a mobile app for scheduling and logging creative practice. It is developed and published by an independent operator based in Quebec, Canada, using the Art Block name in the App Store and related materials.

For privacy law (including who is responsible for decisions about your personal information), the data controller is the same person or business identified as the app’s developer / seller on the Apple App Store listing for Art Block. You can reach that party through the contact options Apple provides there, or as described under Contact below.

Information we collect

Art Block is built for scheduling and logging your own creative practice on your device. There is no Art Block login or account we run for you; what we process is mostly what stays on your phone or tablet, plus limited data needed for subscriptions (see below).

Optional “share” images. If you choose to create a shareable image after a session, that image is generated on your device. It is not posted to us. If you use your device’s share sheet to send it to another app or message, that app’s or platform’s privacy policy applies there, not this one.

There is no separate Art Block login. Profile details, practice logs, and preferences you enter are stored on your device. Paid access is purchased through the App Store; we use RevenueCat to manage subscription validation and entitlements on our behalf, as described under Sharing.

  • Information you provide or generate in the app — for example name or labels you add to your profile, art practice logs, session notes, images you attach to sessions, preferences, and other content you choose to enter (stored locally on your device).
  • Photos and images you choose — if you add a profile photo or attach images (for example to a session), the app accesses your photo library only when you select items; image data is kept in app storage on your device. We do not upload your session images to servers we operate.
  • Information stored on your device — core activity data is stored locally on your device. Platform-level backups (for example iCloud device backup) are governed by your device and Apple’s settings, not by us.
  • Notifications — if you turn on practice reminders, the Service may schedule local notifications on your device. We do not use these for marketing; you can turn them off in the app or in your device settings.
  • Payment-related information — paid features are purchased through the platform (for example Apple’s App Store). We do not receive your full payment card details. We receive limited information about whether your subscription is active. We use RevenueCat, a subscription management provider, which may process identifiers and transaction-related data needed to validate purchases and entitlements; see Sharing and RevenueCat’s privacy documentation.
  • Device and technical data — such as device type, operating system, app version, and diagnostics or crash-related information where allowed by your device settings and the platform. If we add third-party crash or analytics tools, we will describe them here and update this policy before or when they go live.
  • Future features. We may add capabilities later (for example optional accounts, cloud backup or sync, or analytics). If we launch something that stores your content on our servers or with a new vendor, we will update this policy first and describe new collections and providers clearly.

How we use information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service;
  • Manage subscriptions and entitlements (including through RevenueCat);
  • Personalize your experience on your device (for example pursuits, schedules, reminders);
  • Communicate with you about the Service (for example support);
  • Detect, prevent, and address technical or security issues, abuse, or fraud;
  • Comply with law and protect rights, safety, and security.

Storage and international transfers

Your practice content and preferences are intended to stay on your device. If you delete the app or app data using your platform’s controls, locally stored information may be removed, subject to how your device handles backups (for example iCloud). Subscription and entitlement processing through RevenueCat may involve retention on their systems according to their policy and your platform account; that may include servers outside Canada.

If we later offer optional cloud storage or similar, we will explain where data is stored and update this policy before those features collect new categories of information.

We retain personal information only as long as reasonably needed for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer period is required or permitted by law.

Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with:

  • Platform providers — such as Apple, to operate distribution, subscriptions, and platform-level protections.
  • Subscription management (RevenueCat) — we use RevenueCat to validate App Store purchases, manage entitlements, and perform related technical operations. RevenueCat may process identifiers and purchase-related information on their systems. How they handle personal information is described in their privacy policy.
  • Other service providers — today this is mainly your platform (Apple) and RevenueCat. If we add other vendors (for example email delivery or analytics), we will list them here when they apply.
  • Legal or safety — when required by law, legal process, or a proportionate request by public authorities, or to protect users, the public, or the Service.

Your choices and rights

  • You may be able to access, export, correct, or delete certain information from within the app, depending on the feature.
  • You can delete the app or use your device’s settings to clear app data where the Service stores information only on the device.
  • If you are in Canada, including Quebec, you may have rights to access your personal information, correct inaccuracies, withdraw consent where processing is consent-based, and obtain information about how we handle personal information, subject to applicable exceptions. You may also have the right to challenge compliance and, where applicable, to request cessation of dissemination or de-indexation in line with local law.
  • To exercise these rights, contact us as described under Contact. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

Children

The Service is a productivity and journaling style tool for artists; it is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us using the details under Contact and we will take reasonable steps to delete it. Age thresholds and consent rules can vary by region; additional requirements may apply where local law says so.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will post the updated version on this page and change the “Last updated” date above. If we introduce accounts, cloud sync, analytics, or other capabilities that change what we collect, we will describe that here and, where practical, surface a short notice in the app.
